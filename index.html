Our Journey

How It All Began

Long before any of this, there was high school. Two kids on opposite sides of the same friend group — close enough to share a social circle, far enough apart that introductions were apparently not on the agenda. Jeanine noticed Po. Po was, shall we say, otherwise occupied — entangled with someone else in that very same group. A classic case of wrong place, right person, wrong time. The universe, seemingly, had notes.

Fast forward twenty years. Somehow, through the beautiful chaos of life, they found their way back to each other. And naturally, Po — with all the confidence of a man who has completely rewritten history in his own mind — insists that Jeanine simply waited for him. Patiently. Devotedly. For two decades. As one does.

Jeanine has neither confirmed nor denied this theory. The truth, like Po's self-awareness, may never be fully known. What is known is that what started with shared laughter and a love for great food has grown into a lifetime of adventures together — from favorite neighborhood spots to family dinner tables to, now, this. The next chapter. And they want you there for it.

Dogs & Service Animals at the Venue

We know some of you have four-legged family members, and others rely on trained companions — so here's what you need to know about The Great American Beer Hall's policy.

Pet Dogs & Non-Service Animals
Leashed dogs are welcome in the outdoor beer garden and patio only. They are not permitted inside the venue at any time under Massachusetts health department food safety regulations. If you plan to bring your pup to the outdoor areas, keep them leashed, off the furniture, and bring a water bowl — the venue does not provide one.

Certified Service Animals
Fully trained service animals have full access to the entire venue, including indoors, in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Massachusetts state law. Under the ADA, a service animal is a dog (or in rare cases a miniature horse) individually trained to perform specific tasks for a person with a disability. Venue staff may only ask two questions: Is this a service animal required because of a disability? and What task has it been trained to perform? — nothing further.

Emotional Support Animals
Emotional support animals do not carry the same ADA indoor access rights as certified service animals. They are welcome in the outdoor patio areas under the same leash rules as pet dogs, but cannot accompany their owners inside the venue.

Questions? Contact the venue directly at 781-214-0290 or info@gabhall.com, or reach us at potacularwedding@gmail.com.
